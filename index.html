Hey, I'm Becka. My path hasn't been linear, but no matter where my career has taken me, storytelling has always been my superpower.

It started in Buffalo, NY, waking up at 2:30am for the morning news grind. Fueled by coffee and adrenaline, I discovered how important a good story is. I won't shy away from getting out of my comfort zone, especially if it can enhance my stories.

Body of Work

Video Production

Good content doesn't happen by accident. Every piece here started as an idea. Here's how I bring them to life.

Our team overhauled DraftKings' entire onboarding experience from the ground up. Focused on culture, connection, and making new hires feel at home from day one. I owned the planning, scripting, b-roll, and editing.

Our technology is one of our biggest differentiators. I wanted to let the people working with it tell the story. This series puts employees front and center to break down what actually happens behind the scenes on gameday at DraftKings.

What does 'The Crown is Yours' mean to you? The question we asked employees all over the world. Their responses captured exactly who we are. Simple concept, powerful execution.

I own the full production of the DraftKings Life Podcast — concept, booking, recording, and editing. Getting into the studio with leaders and colleagues to pull out real, unscripted stories is one of my favorite parts of the job.

Office tour videos never miss. This brand new space was no exception. I leaned into full MTV Cribs energy to capture the personality of our space and give people a real feel for DraftKings culture.

Our station landed a 30-second local spot during the Super Bowl on Fox — prime real estate. I pitched this piece with a clear goal: make it memorable, witty, and let our news personalities shine in a way that set us apart from the competition. Still one of my favorites.

This one started with a pain point — Siri is notoriously bad at weather. Noticing how similar "Siri" and "Shiri" sounded, I pitched a promo that turned a relatable frustration into a reason to tune in: why ask Siri when Shiri gets it right?

This idea came straight from a viewer. A mom tweeted that her son watched the news just to see the trucks go by during Catherine's weather updates. Since moms were our core morning show target, I ran with it. If we could give kids a reason to tune in, moms would follow. Sometimes the silly ideas are worth experimenting with.

Not every big story is best told by an anchor reading a prompter. I pitched a YouTube series built around the day's most compelling news — short, engaging clips designed to capture an audience beyond the broadcast. Boston is a news-hungry market, and the numbers backed it up. Several videos from this series became top performers on the channel.
